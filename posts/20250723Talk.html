Further Discussion on the Homotopy Category $K(R)$

We present several formal approaches to computing the homotopy category $K(R)$. Here, $R$ may be replaced by a general Abelian category.

The following categories are isomorphic; that is, they possess the same class of objects and naturally isomorphic $\mathrm{Hom}$-sets.

  1. The $H^0(\mathcal{HOM})$-construction. (Observe that the Hom complex is defined for any Abelian category.)
  2. The localisation $C(R) [S^{-1}]$, where $S$ comprises homotopy equivalences, i.e., morphisms $f$ for which there exists a morphism $g$ such that $f âˆ˜ g$ and $g âˆ˜ f$ are homotopic to the identity morphisms.
  3. The additive quotient $C(R) / \mathcal{N}$, where $\mathcal{N}$ consists of split acyclic complexes (that is, contractible complexes).
  4. The homotopy category $C(R) / âˆ¼$, where $âˆ¼$ is an additive categorical equivalence relation on $\mathrm{Hom}$-sets, with $f âˆ¼ 0$ if and only if $f$ is null-homotopic.

We first demonstrate that $C(R) / âˆ¼$ is well-defined. Note that $s : f âˆ¼ g$ if and only if $s âˆ˜ h : f âˆ˜ h âˆ¼ g âˆ˜ h$, and the dual statement holds as well. We denote the category by $K_i$ $(i = 1,2,3,4)$ according to the constructions above.

  1. $(K_1 = K_4)$. This is evident.
  2. $(K_2 = K_4)$. The quotient $C \to K_4$ maps $S$ to isomorphisms, yielding $K_2 \to K_4$. To establish the converse functor, observe that the collection of homotopies $\{(s,f,g) âˆ£ s : f âˆ¼ g : X \to Y\}$ is represented by $(\mathrm{Cyl}(1_X), Y)$ via $Î£ X âŠ• X âŠ• X \xrightarrow {(s,f,g)} Y$.
